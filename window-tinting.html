⚖️ Arizona Tint Laws

Front Windows: Must allow 33% or more light (no darker than 33% VLT)
Back Windows: Any darkness allowed
Windshield: Tint allowed on top 5 inches (AS-1 line) or top 5 inches, whichever is less
We ensure your tint is 100% legal and provide documentation for law enforcement if needed.

Window Tinting FAQs

Common questions about automotive window tint

How long does window tinting take?

Most vehicles take 2-3 hours for complete tinting. We can do it while you wait or at your location if you have other things to do.

Will tint interfere with electronics?

No! Ceramic and carbon tints contain no metal, so they won't interfere with cell phones, GPS, radio, or toll passes.

Can I roll windows down after installation?

We recommend waiting 3 days for the adhesive to fully cure. After that, your windows will function normally.

Is ceramic tint worth the extra cost?

In Arizona, absolutely! Ceramic rejects up to 98% of heat vs 40% for dyed film. The comfort and energy savings pay for the difference quickly.

What if I get pulled over for tint?

We only install legal tints and provide documentation showing VLT%. Front windows will meet Arizona's 33% requirement.

Can you remove old tint?

Yes! We professionally remove old, bubbling, or purple tint. Removal is $25 per window when getting new tint installed.

Do you tint sunroofs and windshields?

Yes! We can tint sunroofs and offer full windshield tinting with clear ceramic that blocks heat without reducing visibility.

What's covered by the lifetime warranty?

The warranty covers peeling, bubbling, cracking, color change, and adhesive failure. It's transferable if you sell the vehicle.

Frequently Asked Questions

Ceramic vs dyed tint — what’s the difference?

Ceramic films block significantly more infrared heat and UV while staying signal‑friendly. Dyed films primarily reduce glare and add privacy.

What about the windshield?

We offer light, legal windshield films and visor strips. We’ll review options during your appointment.

How long before I roll windows down?

Wait 3–5 days (weather dependent) so adhesives fully cure.

Will the film bubble or turn purple?

Quality ceramic films include lifetime warranty against bubbling, peeling, and discoloration.
