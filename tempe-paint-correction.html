Paint Correction FAQs

Common questions about paint correction

How long does paint correction last?

Paint correction permanently removes defects - they don't come back. However, new swirls can develop over time from improper washing. With proper maintenance, results last 2-5 years.

Will paint correction damage my clear coat?

When done properly, no. We measure paint thickness before starting and remove only microns of clear coat. Modern cars have 35-50 microns of clear coat, we typically remove 1-3 microns.

What's the difference between stages?

Stage 1 uses one polishing step for light defects. Stage 2 adds a cutting step for deeper scratches. Stage 3 includes heavy cutting, refining, and finishing for maximum correction.

Can all scratches be removed?

We can remove scratches that don't penetrate through the clear coat. If you can't feel it with your fingernail, we can likely remove it. Deep scratches may require touch-up paint first.

Should I get ceramic coating after?

Absolutely! Paint correction creates the perfect surface for ceramic coating adhesion. The coating then protects your corrected paint from new damage. We offer package discounts.

How often can paint be corrected?

Most vehicles can safely undergo 3-5 full corrections in their lifetime. With paint thickness monitoring, we ensure safe correction levels every time.

What causes swirl marks?

Swirls are micro-scratches caused by improper washing techniques, automatic car washes, dirty towels, or aggressive buffing. Even dealer "details" often create swirls.

Can I wash my car immediately after?

Yes, but we recommend waiting 24 hours for any sealants to fully cure. We'll provide maintenance instructions to keep your paint looking perfect.
