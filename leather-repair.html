Leather Care & Maintenance Tips

Keep your leather looking great after repair

🧹

Regular Cleaning

Clean leather monthly with proper leather cleaner. Avoid harsh chemicals that strip natural oils and cause cracking.

💧

Condition Quarterly

Apply leather conditioner every 3 months to keep leather soft and prevent drying and cracking, especially in Arizona heat.

☀️

UV Protection

Use sunshades or tint windows to reduce UV exposure. Sun is the #1 cause of leather fading and deterioration.

🌡️

Temperature Control

Crack windows when parked to reduce interior heat. Extreme temperatures accelerate leather aging and cracking.

💦

Immediate Cleanup

Clean spills immediately to prevent staining. Blot, don't rub, and use appropriate leather cleaner for best results.

👕

Minimize Friction

Be mindful of rivets, belts, and sharp objects that can scratch or tear leather. Prevention is easier than repair.

Leather Repair FAQs

Common questions about leather repair services

Can all leather damage be repaired?

Most damage can be repaired including tears, cracks, burns, and fading. Extensive damage over 50% of a panel may require replacement. We'll assess and advise honestly.

How long do repairs last?

With proper care, professional repairs can last the life of the vehicle. We use flexible compounds that move with the leather and won't crack or peel.

Will repairs be visible?

Minor repairs are virtually invisible. Larger repairs may show slight texture differences but color will match perfectly. Most people never notice quality repairs.

How much does repair cost?

Small repairs start at $89, while complete seat restoration ranges $300-500. Much cheaper than $2000+ for reupholstering. We provide free quotes.

Do you work on all leather types?

Yes! We repair all automotive leather including perforated, two-tone, and exotic leathers. Also furniture and marine leather.

How long does repair take?

Most repairs take 2-4 hours depending on extent of damage. We work at your location so you can continue your day.

Is repair covered by insurance?

Often yes, especially for damage from accidents or vandalism. We can provide documentation for insurance claims.

Should I try DIY repair first?

We don't recommend it. DIY kits rarely match properly and can make professional repair more difficult. Best to call us first for lasting results.
